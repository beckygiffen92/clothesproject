Colour Palettes

What to consider when picking colours:

The Colour wheel

It's actually very simple, but always refer back to the colour wheel. The colour wheel is used for nearly everything in life, whether its home interiors or graphic design; the colour wheel is always used.

Opposites attract

All these colours sit opposite on the colour wheel, they compliment eachother well which is perfect for those who want a bit of contrast in their outfits.

Monochromatic

Monochromatic means all the same pretty much. These colours are all next to eachother on the colour wheel. They are of the same colour but a different hue. Different hues work well together if you want to keep your outfit all in one colour.

Neutrals

Grey scale and neutral colours, such as browns, camels and rusts naturally compliment one another. A pop of colour in an all black or all white outfit can really make the outfit pop.
